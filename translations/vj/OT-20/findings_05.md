# Findings: Proverbs Chapter 5 Translation

## Requirements
- Translate all 23 verses of Proverbs chapter 5
- Follow Complete Equivalence principle
- Use Vietnamese Protestant 1925 terminology
- Apply GLOSSARY.yaml term mappings

## Research Findings

### Key Terms Analysis
1. **strange woman** - In Proverbs context, refers to adulteress or seductress. Vietnamese: "nguoi phu nu la" or "nguoi phu nu ngoai doi"

2. **wormwood** - Bitter plant, symbol of bitterness and suffering. Vietnamese: "cay dang" or just "dang"

3. **hell** - KJV "sheol" translated as "hell". Vietnamese Protestant: "dia nguc" or "hoa nguc"

4. **cistern** - Water storage, used metaphorically for wife. Vietnamese: "gieng nuoc"

5. **hind/roe** - Deer species, symbols of grace and beauty. Vietnamese: "con huou", "con hoang"

6. **ravished** - Intoxicated, captivated by love. Vietnamese: "say dam"

### Chapter Themes
- Warning against adultery (verses 1-14)
- Faithfulness to one's wife (verses 15-20)
- God's omniscience and judgment (verses 21-23)

## Technical Decisions
| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Use "nguoi phu nu la" for strange woman | Clear foreign/outsider connotation |
| Use "dia nguc" for hell | Standard Protestant term |
| Use "say dam" for ravished | Captures being captivated/infatuated |
